Review

Emerging anti-HIV drugs

May 2005, Vol. 10, No. 2 , Pages 241-274 (doi:10.1517/14728214.10.2.241)
  • PDF (2084 KB)
  • PDF Plus (2110 KB)
  • Reprints
  • Permissions
Erik De Clercq
Rega Institute for Medical Research
K.U.Leuven, Minderbroedersstraat 10, B-3000 Leuven
Belgium



There are now exactly 20 anti-HIV drugs licenced (approved) for clinical use, and > 30 anti-HIV compounds under (pre)clinical development. The licenced anti-HIV drugs fall into five categories: nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NRTIs: zidovudine, didanosine, zalcitabine, stavudine, lamivudine, abacavir and emtricitabine); nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NtRTIs: tenofovir disoproxil fumarate); non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors (NNRTIs: nevirapine, delavirdine and efavirenz); protease inhibitors (PIs: saquinavir, indinavir, ritonavir, nelfinavir, amprenavir, lopinavir, atazanavir and fosamprenavir); and fusion inhibitors (FIs: enfuvirtide). The compounds that are currently under clinical (Phase I, II or III) or preclinical investigation are either targeted at the same specific viral proteins as the licenced compounds (i.e., reverse transcriptase [NRTIs: PSI-5004, (-)-dOTC, DPC-817, elvucitabine, alovudine, MIV-210, amdoxovir, DOT; NNRTIs: thiocarboxanilide, UC-781, capravirine, dapivirine, etravirine, rilpivirine], protease [PIs: tipranavir, TMC-114]) or other specific viral proteins (i.e., gp120: cyanovirin N; attachment inhibitors: AIs, such as BMS-488043; integrase: L-870,812, PDPV-165; capsid proteins: PA-457, α-HCG); or cellular proteins (CD4 downmodulators: CADAs; CXCR4 antagonists: AMD-070, CS-3955; CCR5 antagonists: TAK-220, SCH-D, AK-602, UK-427857). Combination therapy is likely to remain the gold standard for the treatment of AIDS so as to maximise potency, minimise toxicity and diminish the risk for resistance development. Ideally, pill burden should be reduced to once-daily dosing so as to optimise the patient’s compliance and reduce the treatment costs.

Keywords

fusion inhibitors, human immunodeficiency virus, non-nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors, nucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitors, nucleotide reverse transcriptase inhibitors, protease inhibitors

Expert Opinion Editor’s Highlights for May 2013

Expert Opinion on Pharmacotherapy

Pharmacotherapy options for cataplexy

Regis Lopez & Yves Dauvilliers

Narcolepsy is a devastating chronic neurological disorder that disrupts the normal sleep pattern. Cataplexy, which is the sudden loss of voluntary muscular tone brought on by strong emotional factors, is the most specific symptom of the disease and therefore represents the best diagnostic marker. In this excellent review article, Dauvilliers and Lopez discuss the evidence and experience using current treatment strategies and also describe future treatment options

Expert Opinion on Drug Safety

Interactions between oral antineoplastic agents and concomitant medication: a systematic review

Esther Carcelero, Helena Anglada, Montse Tuset & Natalia Creus

In this very timely piece, Carcelero and colleagues explore and summarise the current evidence on these drug interactions, and encourage more clinical interaction studies to be undertaken, to ensure more suitable anticancer pharmacokinetics in clinical practice.

Expert Opinion on Medical Diagnostics

Diagnosis of myalgic encephalomyelitis: where are we now?

Michael Maes, George Anderson, Gerwyn Morris, Michael Berk

The diagnosis of myalgic encephalomyelitis (ME) remains controversial. In this month's issue, Maes and co-authors give us their view on this thorny issue.

Expert Opinion on Orphan Drugs

Emerging therapies and therapeutic concepts for lysosomal storage diseases

Thomas Kirkegaard

In our current issue, Dr Kirkegaard discusses current and future treatments for lysosomal storage diseases and highlights the impact of the Orphan Drug Act on research into these disorders.

Expert Opinion on Drug Delivery

Nanocapsules with specific targeting and release properties using miniemulsion polymerization

Katharina Landfester & Volker Mailander

This review by Landfester et al describes in detail the polymerization process and presents new miniemulsion approaches for designing a variety of nanocapsules including those responsive to stimuli. They discuss various polymers and polymer formations, and various stimuli such as temperature, light and enzymes. Recommendations are made for further investigation into intracellular degradation mechanisms and the interactions between proteins and bodily fluids.

Expert Opinion on Therapeutic Patents

Sigma receptor modulators: a patent review

Collina, Raffaella Gaggeri, Annamaria Marra, Andrea Bassi, Sara Negrinotti, Francesca Negri & Daniela Rossi

Of all receptors, the sigma ones were the least understood until recently when considerable progress has been achieved. This paper is a comprehensive overview of the field, with a review of both scientific and patent literature for many years (starting 1986 until the present time). Both the pharmacology and chemistry for designing sigma receptors modulators is thoroughly reviewed, with the main applications in CNS diseases, neuropathic pain, cancer and neurodegenerative pathologies highlighted.

Expert Opinion on Therapeutic Targets

Challenges in drug target discovery in bipolar disorder

Murtada Alsaif, Frieder Haenisch, Paul C Guest, Hassan Rahmoune & Sabine Bahn

This is a competent, current, well written review of a broad range of intracellular signalling systems relevant to bipolar disorder. The methodological challenges when searching for novel drugs for the treatment of bipolar disorder are discussed, with a special emphasis on what should be taken as key targets and methods to be employed in the near future.

Expert Opinion on Investigational Drugs

Increased antileukemic effects in human acute myeloid leukemia by combining HSP70 and HSP90 inhibitors

Håkon Reikvam, Ina Nepstad, André Sulen, Bjørn Tore Gjertsen, Kimberley Joanne Hatfield & Øystein Bruserud

Heat shock proteins are aberrantly expressed in different malignancies and has emerged as a promising new target for anticancer therapy. Specifically, HSP90 and HSP70 - the second major HSP- have been identified as promising targets for antileukemic therapy.

Expert Opinion on Drug Discovery

Recent applications of isotopic labeling for protein NMR in drug discovery

Hidekazu Hiroaki

In this May issue, the review by Hiroaki considers recent methodological advances in isotopic-labeling methods for protein NMR and evaluates their current and future applicability to guide drug discovery. Given that proteins are targets of most marketed and developing drugs, the paper addresses a topic of great relevance to most contemporary discovery campaigns.

Expert Opinion on Drug Metabolism & Toxicology

Petten Cytochrome P450-mediated metabolism in brain: functional roles and their implications

Ravindranath V, and Strobel HW

The Cytochrome P450 (CYP) enzyme system is among the most studied enzymes especially when considering drug metabolism. However, its role and expression in the brain is not extensively described. In this month’s Editor’s pick, an article by Ravindranith and Strobel examines the distribution and expression of CYP enzymes in the brain of human and non-human species. This review sheds light on the subject and visits the current evidence, discusses specific brain-active drugs, and how alternate splicing has led to discovery of unique CYPs and variants in the brain but not in liver.
